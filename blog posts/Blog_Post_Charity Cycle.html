Giving back and taking stock

170km, 1496m elevation gain, lots of sunburn.

Last January I was still feeling the effects of Covid-19 on my lungs. I struggled to keep up with the kids when playing outside and my asthma was back in a big way. I had also always wanted to cycle the Ring of Kerry, having grown up seeing the cycle in my home town. So I figured, two birds with one stone.

I spoke to my local VP, convinced him to have the company cover entrance fees to the annual Ring of Kerry Charity Cycle, bought a bike and set about training for the cycle in the coming July.

The cycle gave us a platform in our company to talk about the importance of active commuting and enabled us to highlight various ways in which people could get back into fitness in the new year. We also had a show case of bicycle providers to help the team take advantage of a local government scheme which provides tax back on the purchase of a bicycle.

During the cycle we sped down sweeping views of the atlantic, climbed grueling hills but managed to avoid any punctures! The funds I raised were to support Breakthrough Cancer Research helping to find new ways to treat and detect cancers. The cycle overall was a great success, not only personally but it helped foster a culture of activity in our small company which has turned into our annual fitness challenge!

To top it all off we had an evening in Killarney, staying in a pub turned Air BnB and I got myself some fresh new ink to celebrate, which was great given how dead my legs were I didn't feel a thing!
